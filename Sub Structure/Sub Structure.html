Substitutions of Structure

This piece focused on structures of language, as well as engaging the viewer in content creation and semantic development. Using laser-cut wooden glyphs as units of meaning to stand in for specific parts of speech, viewers constructed sentences by connecting the glyphs with pieces of string. Each contribution to the structure helped develop the semantic logic of the greater glyph ‘language.’ After contributing, the participant was awarded a token etched with one of the glyphs as a way of spreading the represented linguistic system and rewarding contribution.

Substitutions of Structure was part of the group interactive exhibition Nonversation and was produced in collaboration with Davis Scherer and Bex Rohlk.
